# Week 1 – Problem Statement Document  
## Public Transport Delay Predictor  

---

## 1. Problem Definition  
Public transport is widely used in Australia, especially in major cities like Sydney and Melbourne. However, delays in buses, trains, and other services are common due to factors such as traffic congestion, weather conditions, and operational issues.  

Most existing transport applications only provide **real-time updates**, meaning users are informed **after a delay has already occurred**. This creates inconvenience as commuters cannot plan ahead or avoid disruptions.  

The main problem is the lack of a system that can **predict delays before they happen**, allowing users to make better travel decisions.  

---

## 2. Target Users  
The problem mainly affects:  

- Daily commuters (students, workers)  
- Public transport users (bus, train, metro passengers)  
- Tourists unfamiliar with transport systems  
- Transport planners and authorities  

These users rely heavily on timely transport and are negatively impacted by unexpected delays.  

---

## 3. Australian Market Context  
In Australia, public transport systems are heavily used in urban areas like Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane. With growing population and traffic congestion, delays are becoming more frequent.  

Although apps like Transport for NSW and Google Maps provide real-time updates, they lack **predictive features** that help users anticipate delays in advance.  

This gap creates an opportunity for a smarter, data-driven solution that aligns with Australia’s smart city initiatives and improves commuter experience.  

---

## 4. Project Vision  
The vision of this project is to develop a **web-based Public Transport Delay Predictor** that uses historical and real-time data to:  

- Predict possible delays before they occur  
- Provide users with delay insights and probabilities  
- Help users plan alternative routes or travel times  
- Improve overall travel efficiency and experience  

The system aims to make public transport more **reliable, predictable, and user-friendly**.  

---

## 5. Key Questions Answered  

### What problem are we solving?  
We are solving the problem of **unpredictable public transport delays** by providing early predictions instead of only real-time updates.  

### Who experiences this problem?  
Daily commuters, students, workers, tourists, and anyone using public transport in Australia.  

### Why is it important in Australia?  
Public transport is essential in major Australian cities, and delays impact productivity, time management, and commuter satisfaction. A predictive system can significantly improve travel planning and efficiency.  

---

## Summary  
Public transport users in Australia face frequent delays, but current apps only show updates after delays occur. This project aims to predict delays in advance using data, helping users plan better and reduce travel disruptions.
