## 4. Market Analysis  

Public transport usage in Australia is increasing, especially in major cities like Sydney and Melbourne. Commuters depend heavily on mobile applications to plan their journeys.  

Current solutions mainly focus on:
- Real-time tracking  
- Route planning  
- Service alerts  

However, these systems are **reactive**, meaning they only respond after delays happen.  

There is a growing demand for **smart and predictive systems** as part of smart city development. A system that predicts delays can significantly improve commuter experience and efficiency.  

---

## 5. Unsolved Problems (Market Gaps)  

The following problems are still not solved by current competitors:  

- ❌ No prediction of delays before they occur  
- ❌ Limited use of historical data for insights  
- ❌ Lack of proactive alerts (before disruption)  
- ❌ Limited personalized delay recommendations  
- ❌ No clear delay probability or risk indication  

---

## 6. Opportunity for Our Project  

Our Public Transport Delay Predictor will address these gaps by:  

- Providing **early delay predictions**  
- Using **historical + real-time data analysis**  
- Giving **delay probability (risk level)**  
- Offering **smart suggestions for alternative routes**  

This creates a more **proactive and intelligent transport system** compared to existing solutions.  

---

## 7. Conclusion  

While current transport apps provide useful real-time information, they fail to predict delays in advance. This creates a strong opportunity for a predictive system that improves planning, reduces uncertainty, and enhances commuter satisfaction.  

Our project aims to fill this gap by delivering a smarter, data-driven solution for public transport users in Australia.
